# A Letter to the People of Ghana

To the farmers, families, and communities of Ghana —

We are writing to you because Ghana matters to what we are building.

Not because Ghana is a market. Not because Ghana is a test case. But because the land problem in Ghana is real, it has been real for a long time, and the people who have tried to solve it before have failed you. You deserve to know that we understand that history — and that we are building something different.

---

## What We Know About Ghana

We know that approximately 78% of the land in Ghana has no formal registration. We know that families have farmed the same land for generations with no deed, no title, no document that a court or a bank would recognize as proof.

We know that the government's Land Administration Project ran for seventeen years and was undermined by the very corruption it was supposed to address.

We know that Bitland launched in 2014 with promises of a blockchain solution — and more than ten years later, those promises have not been kept.

We know that the people who have suffered most from these failures are not powerful people. They are farmers. They are women. They are families in communities where everyone knows who owns what land — but no system has ever written it down in a way that protects them.

We are not here to make more promises. We are here to show you something real.

---

## What Landos Is

Landos is a land ownership system built on one simple principle: your ownership should not depend on any government, institution, or powerful person being willing to recognize it.

When you register your land on Landos, your community's knowledge becomes a permanent record. Your neighbors confirm your claim. Your presence on your land builds proof over time. And that record is stored on thousands of computers around the world simultaneously — computers that no single government, company, or official controls.

A corrupt official cannot delete it. A regime change cannot erase it. A bribe cannot alter it.

It simply exists. And it will keep existing.

---

## What We Are Not

We are not another NGO that will arrive, conduct workshops, and disappear.

We are not asking the Ghanaian government for permission to help Ghanaian people. We are not waiting for institutions to approve a system designed to protect people from those same institutions.

We are not a cryptocurrency scheme. We are not asking anyone to invest money or take financial risk.

We are building infrastructure — the same way roads and phone networks are infrastructure. Something that works for everyone, that belongs to no one, and that will still be here long after any organization involved in building it is gone.

---

## What This Costs You

Nothing.

Registering your land on Landos costs nothing. We have designed the system specifically so that the people who need it most are never stopped by a fee they cannot afford.

---

## What We Are Asking

We are asking for something harder than money. We are asking for your attention, your questions, and — if what we are building proves worthy of it — your trust.

We know trust is not given easily. Not after everything that has been tried and failed. We do not expect it on the first day. We expect to earn it the same way any record earns trust — over time, through consistency, through proving that what we say is true.

Ghana will be one of the first places in the world where Landos comes to life. That is not an accident. The land problem is urgent here. The community knowledge is rich here. And the people here have waited long enough.

We are ready to work alongside you.

---

With respect,

**The Landos Team**

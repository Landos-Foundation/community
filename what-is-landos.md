# What is Landos?

Landos is a system that lets people prove they own their land — without relying on a government, a bank, or any institution to keep that record for them.

---

## The Problem

Imagine you have lived on the same piece of land your whole life. Your parents lived there before you. Your grandparents before them. Everyone in your community knows it is yours.

But there is no document that proves it.

No deed. No title. No entry in a government registry. Just the fact that you are there, and people around you know it.

Now imagine someone more powerful than you decides they want that land. A government official. A developer. A corporation. Without a formal record, they can claim the land is theirs — and you have nothing to show that it isn't.

This is not a rare situation. It affects hundreds of millions of people around the world — farmers, families, indigenous communities — people who have lived on land for generations but have no legal proof of ownership. When someone more powerful wants their land, they lose it. Because the record doesn't exist, or because whoever controls the record can be bribed to change it.

**The problem is simple: land ownership is controlled by whoever controls the registry. And registries can be corrupted.**

---

## The Idea Behind Landos

In 2008, a system called Bitcoin was created. Bitcoin solved a problem that seemed impossible: how do you create a record that no single person or institution controls, that cannot be altered, and that anyone in the world can verify?

Bitcoin did this for money. Landos does it for land.

When you register your land on Landos, a permanent record is created. That record is stored on thousands of computers around the world simultaneously. No single government, company, or person controls it. No official can delete it. No bribe can change it. It simply exists — and it always will.

Your land record on Landos is yours. Not because an institution grants it to you. Because it is written into a system that belongs to no one.

---

## How It Works — Simply

When you register your land on Landos:

**Your location is recorded.** Every piece of land on Earth has a unique identifier on Landos — like a permanent address that belongs to no government and no company.

**Your neighbors confirm it.** The people around you — people who know you, who know your land — sign off on your claim. Their confirmation is part of your record. This is how Landos encodes something governments often ignore: communities know who lives where.

**You prove you are there.** Over time, your physical presence on your land adds to your record. The longer you have been there, the stronger your claim.

**The record is permanent.** Once confirmed, your land record cannot be altered or erased. It is anchored to Bitcoin — one of the most permanent records in the world — so it will exist long after any organization or government that might want to take it.

---

## What Landos Is Not

**It is not a cryptocurrency scheme.** Landos uses technology developed for cryptocurrency, but it is not a trading platform or a speculation tool. It is a record-keeping system for land.

**It is not a government project.** Landos does not ask governments for permission to exist. It runs alongside existing systems as an independent, incorruptible record.

**It is not controlled by a company.** The Landos Foundation handles some practical work in the early years — supporting communities, answering questions, building trust. But it does not control the system. No one does.

**It is not only for technical people.** You do not need to understand how it works to use it. You need access to a phone, people in your community who know you, and the time to walk through a simple process.

---

## Who It Is For

Landos is built for the people who need it most.

The farmer in West Africa whose land has been in his family for four generations but has no deed.

The woman in Southeast Asia who built her home on land that the government does not recognize as hers.

The indigenous community in South America whose territory has been occupied for centuries but whose boundaries exist only in memory and oral tradition.

The family in a city neighborhood that has grown up informally — where everyone knows who lives where, but no official record agrees.

These are the people Landos exists to serve. Not investors. Not institutions. Not people who already have working land registries.

---

## Why It Matters

In 2000, the economist Hernando de Soto estimated that the world's poor hold approximately **$9.3 trillion** in land and assets that cannot be used economically — because they have no formal recognition. This land cannot be used as collateral for a loan. It cannot attract investment. It cannot be formally sold or passed to children. It simply exists, valuable but locked.

A land record changes that. The moment your ownership is formally recognized — even outside government systems — your land becomes something you can build on economically, not just physically.

Landos does not just protect land. It unlocks it.

## What Happens Next

Landos is still being built. No network exists yet. What exists is a design — a careful, principled plan for how this system should work.

That design is being developed openly, with input from anyone who wants to contribute.

# What Happens When You Register

Registering your land on Landos is not like filing paperwork with a government office. There is no form to submit, no official to approve your application, no fee to pay, and no waiting for someone in an office to decide whether your claim is valid.

It is a process driven by your community — and by the simple fact of your presence on your land.

Here is what it looks like.

---

## Before You Start

You need three things:

**A phone.** It does not have to be yours. You need access to a smartphone with a data connection long enough to complete the registration — even briefly.

**Your location.** The Landos app uses your phone's GPS to identify the land you are registering. You should be on or near your land when you do this.

**Your neighbors.** At least a few people who live near you and know your land will need to participate. They need their own Landos accounts. If they do not have one yet, they can create one — the process is the same.

---

## Step 1: Create Your Account

Your Landos account is tied to a digital identity that belongs only to you. No one else can use it. No one can take it away.

You verify who you are through a simple identity check — the same kind used by many apps and services today. Landos stores only a confirmation that you passed this check, never your personal details.

Your account is yours. It is secured by a private key — a unique code that only you hold. This is like the key to a safe. Keep it somewhere safe. Landos provides a recovery system in case you lose it.

---

## Step 2: Define Your Land

Using the Landos app, you mark the boundaries of your land. This can be done by walking the edges of your property while the app records your path, or by drawing boundaries on a map.

Every piece of land on Landos has a unique identifier based purely on its location on Earth — not on any government numbering system, not on any political boundary. Your land's identifier belongs to the geography, not to any institution.

---

## Step 3: Your Neighbors Confirm

You invite your neighbors to confirm your claim. They review the boundaries you have drawn and, if they agree that this is your land, they sign off using their own accounts.

Their confirmation is permanent. It becomes part of your record. Multiple neighbors signing independently is much stronger than any single document — because it represents the collective knowledge of the people who know the truth.

---

## Step 4: Your Claim Builds Over Time

Registration is not a single moment — it is the beginning of a record that grows stronger over time.

As you continue to live on your land, the system tracks your presence. Regular check-ins from your location add to your habitation record. More neighbor attestations can be added. Community members can write statements about your land's history.

A claim that was registered last week is newer and less certain than a claim that has been building for five years. The longer you participate, the stronger your record becomes.

---

## Step 5: Your Claim Is Confirmed

When enough evidence has accumulated — enough neighbor confirmations, enough habitation record, enough community attestation — your claim moves to Confirmed status.

A confirmed claim is the strongest form of land record on Landos. It means that multiple independent sources have verified your ownership, that you have demonstrated physical presence over time, and that your community stands behind your claim.

---

## What Your Record Looks Like

Your land record on Landos contains:

- The precise location and boundaries of your land
- The date your claim was first registered
- The attestations of your neighbors, signed with their identities
- Your habitation history — evidence of your presence over time
- Any written statements from community members
- A complete history of every change ever made to the record

This record is visible to anyone in the world who wants to verify it. But your personal identity — your name, your government ID, your biometric data — is never part of the public record. What the world sees is proof that your claim is legitimate. Not who you are.

---

## What Happens If Someone Challenges Your Claim

If someone files a competing claim on your land, the system is designed to protect you.

You are notified immediately. A waiting period begins — nothing can happen without you having time to respond. The challenger must post a financial deposit to proceed. If their claim is found to be made in bad faith, they lose that deposit.

The people who confirmed your claim — your neighbors — are part of your defense. The years of habitation evidence are part of your defense. The community's memory is part of your defense.

A fraudulent challenge is expensive to make and likely to fail. A legitimate dispute is handled through a fair process that weighs all the evidence.

---

## You Do Not Have to Understand All of This

The steps above describe what the system does. Most of it happens in the background. What you experience is a simple process guided by the app — take this step, then this one, then this one.

You do not need to understand cryptography. You do not need to understand blockchain. You need to know your land, your neighbors, and how to follow instructions on a phone.

That is enough.
